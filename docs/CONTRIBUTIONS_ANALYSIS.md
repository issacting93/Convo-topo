## 1. What Are Your Key Contributions?

Based on your documentation, you have **three primary contributions** that work together:

### Contribution 1: METHODOLOGICAL
**A way of staging encounters with relational patterns that typically remain invisible**

**What this means:**
- You offer a **method** for making relational dynamics visible through spatial/topographic encoding
- The terrain metaphor provides a representational device that leverages spatial affordances to reveal patterns
- You demonstrate how to use PAD (Pleasure-Arousal-Dominance) model to encode emotional intensity as terrain height
- You show how role-based positioning (X/Y axes) and affective intensity (Z-axis) work together to reveal relational drift

**Why it matters:**
- Current interfaces (linear transcripts) cannot reveal these patterns
- Your method makes visible: frustration peaks preceding role inversions, affiliation valleys marking smooth coordination, spatial clustering revealing interactional patterns
- The method is **reusable** - others could apply this approach to visualize relational dynamics in other contexts

**Key phrase**: "A way of staging encounters with patterns that typically remain invisible"

---

### Contribution 2: SUBSTANTIVE  
**Demonstrates how conversational AI mediates relations beyond dyadic exchanges**

**What this means:**
- Your visualization reveals how authority, agency, and control are **distributed** through interactional positioning (not fixed in human or AI)
- You show how individual interactions participate in **broader patterns** (sociotechnical, cultural, political)
- You make visible how relational configurations **accumulate and transform** over time
- You reveal how affective states (PAD) participate in relational configurations

**Why it matters:**
- Responds to DIS 2026's call to move "beyond dyadic human-system relations"
- Reveals political dimensions (power distribution) often hidden in technical systems
- Shows how conversational AI participates in broader ecosystems of relations

**Key phrase**: "Conversational AI technologies mediate relations that extend far beyond the dyadic human-system exchange"

---

### Contribution 3: THEORETICAL/REFRAMING
**Reframes interactivity as relational positioning and affective intensity rather than input-output**

**What this means:**
- You reframe interactivity from "user input → system response" to **relational positioning** (X/Y axes) and **affective intensity** (Z-axis via PAD)
- You treat conversation as **observable structure** rather than inferred psychology
- You show how interactivity is about **entangled systems** (sociotechnical, cultural, temporal) rather than isolated dyads
- You use **moment-to-moment emotional responses** (PAD per message) rather than accumulated sentiment

**Why it matters:**
- Challenges dominant HCI models of interactivity
- Provides theoretical framework grounded in communication theory (Goffman's footing, Watzlawick's relational communication)
- Shows how PAD model can be used for visualization (not just detection) of affective patterns over time

**Key phrase**: "Reframes interactivity as relational positioning and affective intensity"

## 5. Revised Contribution Statement (For Abstract/Conclusion)

**For Abstract (150 words):**

"We present Conversational Topography, an interactive system that makes visible relational dynamics in human-LLM conversations through 3D terrain visualization. Our contribution is threefold: **(1) Methodological** - we offer a way of staging encounters with relational patterns (frustration peaks, affiliation valleys, spatial clustering) that remain invisible in linear transcripts, using PAD-based affective encoding and role-based positioning; **(2) Substantive** - we demonstrate how conversational AI mediates relations beyond dyadic exchanges, revealing distributed authority, accumulated patterns, and sociotechnical entanglements; **(3) Theoretical** - we reframe interactivity as relational positioning (X/Y axes) and affective intensity (Z-axis via PAD) rather than input-output cycles, treating conversation as observable structure rather than inferred psychology. Grounded in Goffman's footing and relational communication theory, the system invites reflection on what should—and should not—be made legible in AI-mediated relationships."

**For Conclusion (Concise Version):**

"Our contributions are methodological (a way of staging encounters with invisible patterns), substantive (revealing how conversational AI mediates relations beyond dyadic exchanges), and theoretical (reframing interactivity as relational positioning and affective intensity). Together, they demonstrate how interactive technologies can illuminate relational ecosystems while engaging critically with the interpretive risks of making the invisible visible."
