**Abstract (‚â§150 words)**
Human‚ÄìAI conversations are traditionally analyzed as text transcripts or metrics, obscuring relational dynamics that unfold over time. We introduce *Conversation Cartographer*, a visual system that renders dialogue as trajectories through a three-dimensional relational space defined by functional‚Äìsocial orientation, linguistic alignment, and affective intensity. By encoding each turn as a coordinate and constructing continuous paths, the map reveals patterns of relational drift, emotional change, and role positioning that are invisible in surface statistics. The visual terrain and clustered landscapes enable designers and researchers to see, compare, and interpret conversational journeys, supporting more transparent and role-aware interactive systems. Substantive visuals are central to conveying these design insights.
