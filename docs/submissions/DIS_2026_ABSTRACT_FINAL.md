## Abstract (150 words) ✅

What relational possibilities are foreclosed when human–AI interaction collapses into instrumental tool-use? **Conversational Cartography** maps 562 analyzed conversations as movement through relational-affective space, encoding social role orientation, structural alignment, and emotional intensity. Using a 12-role Social Role Theory taxonomy reveals that interactions with identical role pairings take dramatically different trajectories (82x variance), yet clustering identifies only 7 relational positioning patterns—trajectory features drive 83.3% of cluster separation. Role network analysis of 562 conversations reveals that 97% of interactions are instrumental (information-seeking/providing, task-directing), with only 3% expressive engagement—yet these rare expressive attempts receive instrumental responses (Expert-System 65%), suggesting current paradigms successfully optimize for information exchange while foreclosing relational possibilities.

The visualization externalizes this constraint as navigable terrain: most cluster in narrow functional corridors revealing what's missing. Using GPT-5.2 to classify AI-mediated interactions creates methodological circularity, exposing how AI systems interpret their own relational possibilities. It challenges dyadic models, staging encounters with political dimensions of how we position ourselves—and are positioned—in relationships. It is not diagnostic but critical—a tool for confronting what current interaction paradigms foreclose.

## Role Network Evidence (Updated with Full Dataset)

### Actual Distribution (n=562 conversations with role assignments):

**Human Roles:**
- Provider: **44.5%** (250) - Users sharing information, teaching AI
- Director: **27.9%** (157) - Users commanding, directing tasks
- Information-Seeker: **24.6%** (138) - Users asking questions
- Social-Expressor: **3.0%** (17) - Users sharing experiences ← RARE

**AI Roles:**
- Expert-System: **65.1%** (366) - AI delivering facts, information
- Advisor: **19.2%** (108) - AI providing guidance
- Co-Constructor: **6.4%** (36) - AI collaborating
- Social-Facilitator: **4.1%** (23) - AI reflecting emotions
- Relational-Peer: **3.7%** (21) - AI engaging as equal

**Top Role Pairings:**
1. Provider → Expert-System: **33.1%** (186) - Human teaches, AI responds with facts
2. Information-Seeker → Expert-System: **20.8%** (117) - Traditional Q&A
3. Director → Expert-System: **11.0%** (62) - Human commands, AI delivers
4. Provider → Advisor: **9.6%** (54) - Human seeks guidance
5. Director → Advisor: **6.9%** (39) - Human directs, AI advises

**Key Insight:**
- Humans are INSTRUMENTAL (97%) in actual usage
- AI functions as EXPERT-SYSTEM (65%) not facilitator
- Social/expressive interactions are RARE (3%) in the wild
- **The foreclosure is visible**: Even when users attempt expressive engagement (3%), AI responds instrumentally (Expert-System/Advisor)
- **Question for research**: Is this user preference, or system constraint?
